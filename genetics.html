Genetics Experiment

In my genetics experiment, “Biotech Breakthroughs” I learned a variety of things. I learned about PCR, DNA extraction, how to manage Adobe Premier and create different types of cuts in my videos. In the beginning of our experiment I was taught about PCR and how it is used in the modern world. PCR stands for Polymerase Chain Reaction. It is a technique that makes millions of copies of a specific DNA segment from a sample. PCR is widely used for medicine, biology and forensic science. There are three basic steps to PCR: Denaturation, annealing, and extension. In short, these steps change the temperature of DNA to make it susceptible to cloning. I was also taught the importance of DNA extraction in PCR. DNA extraction is the process of removing DNA from cells to study and test in labs. Without properly extracting DNA, using PCR as we did is impossible. Overall, I learned many things in my genetics project that will help me have a better understanding in modern day science.
